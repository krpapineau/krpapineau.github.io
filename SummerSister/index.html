Who are we?

The Summer Sister Music Festival is a celebration of women in music and serves to recognize established and emerging female artists. 2020 will be the festival's inaugural year, held from June 5th through 7th, in Washington Park just outside Downtown Portland. Summer Sister aims to recreate the quintessential, nostalgic camp experience for people of all ages, backgrounds, and identities, with musical performances, interactive tents, and art exhibits.

The venue

Washington Park covers over 400 acres and is a public urban park in Portland, Oregon. It includes a zoo, forestry museum, arboretum, children's museum, rose garden, Japanese garden, amphitheatre, memorials, archery range, tennis courts, soccer field, picnic areas, playgrounds, public art and many acres of wild forest with miles of trails.

The Cause

Summer Sister is run in partnership with the Girls Rock Camp Alliance, supporting music programs for young women around the world. The GRCA is an international membership network of youth-centered arts and social justice organizations, providing resources and spaces for community building in order to build a strong movement for collective liberation. With youth music and arts programs losing funding, it is important to give young women access to specially funded programs and opportunities to promote creative expression and technical skills.
